## The gap / wedge

**Hypothesis (unvalidated):** an *agent* that closes the loop — plan → draft on-brand → format per platform → publish on cadence, with a human-approval gate — at non-profit-friendly pricing. Schedulers stop at scheduling; AI writers stop at drafting; agencies are too expensive. Whether this gap is real and valued is **unknown — needs discovery** (see `brief.md` §6, assumptions 1–2).

## Risks & unknowns

- **Platform dependency:** publishing depends on third-party social APIs (auth, app review, rate limits, content policy) — a material feasibility and cost risk for a "non-profit pricing" promise.
- **Brand-safety / trust:** auto-published AI content risks off-brand or unsafe posts; may force heavy human review, eroding the "agent" value.
- **Incumbency:** established schedulers + general AI tools already occupy adjacent ground; differentiation must be real, not cosmetic.
- **Unit economics:** LLM + multi-platform integration cost vs. non-profit price point is unproven.
